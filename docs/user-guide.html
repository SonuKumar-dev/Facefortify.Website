User Guide

Everything you need to know to use FaceFortify

🚀 Getting Started

First Launch

When you launch FaceFortify for the first time:

  1. The app will ask for camera permission - click Allow/Yes
  2. You'll see the landing page with a "Get Started" button
  3. Click "Get Started" to begin registration

Enter Your Name

You'll be asked to enter your name. This is just for display - it doesn't affect security.

💡 Tip: Use your real name so you remember which profile you created.

👤 Registering Your Face

What Happens During Registration

FaceFortify will capture 30 frames of your face to create a secure profile. This takes about 30-60 seconds.

Best Practices for Registration

  • ✅ Good Lighting: Face a window or light source. Avoid backlighting.
  • ✅ Face the Camera: Look directly at the camera, keep your head straight.
  • ✅ Stay in Frame: Keep your face inside the green rectangle.
  • ✅ Stay Still: Don't move too much, but slight movements are okay.
  • ✅ Remove Obstructions: Take off sunglasses or hats that cover your face.
  • ✅ Be Natural: Normal expression, you can wear regular glasses.

What You'll See

  • Green Rectangle: Shows detected face
  • Cyan Circles: Shows detected eyes (both eyes should be visible)
  • Confidence Score: Should be 80%+ for good detection
  • Progress Counter: "Capturing... 15/30 frames"
⚠️ Important: Both eyes must be detected for successful registration. If you wear glasses, make sure there's no glare on the lenses.

Common Registration Issues

  • "No face detected": Move closer to camera or improve lighting
  • "No eyes detected": Face camera directly, remove sunglasses
  • Frame count not increasing: Stay still and ensure good lighting
✅ Success: Once 30 frames are captured, your face is registered and you can start locking folders!

🔑 Setting a Backup Password

Why Set a Backup Password?

Your main encryption key is tied to:

  • Your face (what you are)
  • Your computer's hardware (what you have)

If you change your computer or upgrade hardware (new motherboard, CPU, etc.), you won't be able to unlock your folders!

When to Set It

  • ✅ Recommended for everyone - Better safe than sorry
  • ✅ Essential if: You plan to upgrade your PC
  • ✅ Essential if: You use a laptop that might need repairs
  • ❌ Skip if: You use a desktop PC that never changes AND you accept the risk

How to Set It

  1. Go to Settings (gear icon)
  2. Find "Backup Password" section
  3. Click "Set Backup Password"
  4. Enter a strong password
  5. Confirm the password
  6. Store it safely in a password manager!
⚠️ Warning: If you lose both your hardware AND your backup password, your data is permanently lost. There is NO recovery method. This is by design for maximum security.
💡 Password Tips:
  • Use at least 12 characters
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols
  • Store in a password manager (LastPass, 1Password, Bitwarden)
  • Don't use the same password you use elsewhere

🔒 Locking Folders

Adding Folders

  1. Click "Add Folder" on the main screen
  2. Browse to the folder you want to protect
  3. Select it and click "Select Folder"
  4. The folder appears in your list

Locking Your Folders

  1. Click "Lock All" button
  2. The camera window opens for face verification
  3. Look at the camera (only takes 10 frames, ~10 seconds)
  4. Once verified, all folders are encrypted
💡 What Happens When You Lock:
  • Every file in the folder is encrypted with AES-256
  • Access permissions are removed (can't open without unlocking)
  • Files appear as encrypted data if opened
  • You must unlock to access files again

What You Can Lock

  • ✅ Personal documents
  • ✅ Photos and videos
  • ✅ Work files
  • ✅ Financial records
  • ✅ Any folder with sensitive data
⚠️ Don't Lock These:
  • ❌ System folders (Windows, Program Files, etc.)
  • ❌ Your entire user folder
  • ❌ Folders with actively running programs
  • ❌ Shared network folders

Lock Time

Locking time depends on folder size:

  • Small folder (100 MB): ~5 seconds
  • Medium folder (1 GB): ~30 seconds
  • Large folder (10 GB): ~3-5 minutes

🔓 Unlocking Folders

How to Unlock

  1. Click "Unlock All" button
  2. Camera opens for face verification
  3. Look at the camera (10 frames)
  4. Once verified, folders are decrypted and accessible

Verification Process

During unlock, FaceFortify:

  • Captures 10 frames of your face
  • Compares with your registered face profile
  • Needs 60% match rate (6 out of 10 frames)
  • Verifies both eyes are detected
💡 Quick Unlock Tips:
  • Face the camera directly
  • Use similar lighting as registration
  • Wear similar glasses (or none) as registration
  • Keep face in the green box

If Verification Fails

If your face isn't recognized:

  1. Try again with better lighting
  2. Use backup password (if you set one):
    • Click "Use Backup Password" option
    • Enter your password
    • Folders will unlock without face scan
  3. Re-register your face if you've changed significantly
⚠️ No Backup Password? If face verification fails and you don't have a backup password, you cannot unlock your folders. Make sure to set a backup password!

📁 Managing Your Folders

Removing Folders from List

  1. Make sure the folder is unlocked first
  2. Click the "Remove" button next to the folder
  3. Confirm removal
⚠️ Important: You must unlock a folder before removing it from the list. If you remove a locked folder, it stays encrypted and you'll need to add it back to unlock it.

Checking Folder Status

Each folder shows its current status:

  • 🔒 Locked - Encrypted and protected
  • 🔓 Unlocked - Accessible normally

Individual vs. All Folders

  • "Lock All": Locks all unlocked folders at once
  • "Unlock All": Unlocks all locked folders at once
  • Individual buttons: Lock or unlock specific folders

⚙️ Settings & Preferences

Available Settings

User Profile

  • Change Name: Update your display name
  • Re-register Face: Update your face profile if you've changed significantly
  • View Statistics: See login count and last login time

Security

  • Set/Change Backup Password: Create or update your recovery password
  • View Encryption Info: See technical details about your encryption

Data Location

  • Windows: %APPDATA%\FaceFortify
  • Linux: ~/.local/share/FaceFortify
  • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/FaceFortify
💡 Tip: You can backup these folders to preserve your settings, but remember they contain encrypted data tied to your specific hardware!

✨ Best Practices

Security Best Practices

  • ✅ Set a backup password and store it securely
  • ✅ Lock your computer when you leave it (Win+L on Windows)
  • ✅ Use a strong OS password - FaceFortify adds security, but OS login is your first line of defense
  • ✅ Keep your OS updated for security patches
  • ✅ Don't share your computer while folders are unlocked

Usage Best Practices

  • ✅ Unlock when needed, lock when done - Don't keep sensitive folders unlocked all the time
  • ✅ Backup important files before encrypting (to external drive or cloud)
  • ✅ Test unlock after first locking to ensure everything works
  • ✅ Keep good lighting for reliable face recognition

What NOT to Do

  • ❌ Don't lock system folders or program files
  • ❌ Don't delete the FaceFortify data folder manually
  • ❌ Don't lock folders that are being used by running programs
  • ❌ Don't forget your backup password!

🔧 Troubleshooting

Face Not Recognized

Solutions:

  • Improve lighting - face a window or light
  • Remove sunglasses or heavy makeup
  • Face the camera directly, not at an angle
  • If you've changed significantly (new beard, haircut), re-register your face
  • Use backup password if available

Locked Folder Won't Open

Solutions:

  • Make sure you clicked "Unlock" successfully
  • Verify the folder status shows "Unlocked"
  • Try unlocking again
  • Restart FaceFortify and try again

Hardware Changed - Can't Unlock

Solutions:

  • Use your backup password to unlock
  • If you don't have a backup password, your data cannot be recovered
  • This is a security feature, not a bug

Camera Not Working

Solutions:

  • Check camera works in other apps
  • Grant camera permission in OS settings
  • Restart FaceFortify
  • Restart your computer
